Mahatma Gandhi (1869-1948) - The Great Soul Who Changed the World Through Peace

The Apostle of Non-Violence

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, known as Mahatma Gandhi, was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule. His philosophy of satyagraha (truth-force) and ahimsa (non-violence) inspired civil rights and freedom movements across the world.

Born in British-ruled India, Gandhi first employed nonviolent civil disobedience as an expatriate lawyer in South Africa. Upon returning to India, he organized peasants, farmers, and urban laborers to protest excessive land-tax and discrimination. Leading the Indian National Congress, Gandhi championed the cause of independence through peaceful resistance, fasting, and civil disobedience, proving that moral force could triumph over physical force.

Mahatma Gandhi proved that the greatest victories can be won without violence, that truth and moral courage are more powerful than armies and weapons. His philosophy of non-violence and civil disobedience has inspired countless freedom fighters and social reformers around the world. From Martin Luther King Jr.'s civil rights movement to Nelson Mandela's fight against apartheid, Gandhi's principles continue to guide those who seek justice and equality. His life reminds us that ordinary people can achieve extraordinary change through dedication to truth and non-violence.
