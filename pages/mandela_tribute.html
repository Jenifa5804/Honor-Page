Nelson Mandela (1918-2013) - The Freedom Fighter Who United a Nation

The Freedom Fighter

Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary, political leader, and philanthropist who served as President of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. He was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully representative democratic election. His government focused on dismantling the legacy of apartheid by tackling institutionalised racism and fostering racial reconciliation.

Ideologically an African nationalist and socialist, he served as the president of the African National Congress (ANC) party from 1991 to 1997. Internationally, Mandela was the secretary-general of the Non-Aligned Movement from 1998 to 1999. A Xhosa, Mandela was born into the Thembu royal family in Mvezo, British South Africa. Mandela became an icon of resistance to apartheid and a symbol of the struggle for freedom and human rights worldwide.

Journey to Freedom

1918
Birth in Mvezo

Born into the Thembu royal family in the Eastern Cape of South Africa.

1944
Joins ANC

Joined the African National Congress and co-founded the ANC Youth League.

1955
Freedom Charter

Helped draft the Freedom Charter, envisioning a democratic and equal South Africa.

1964
Rivonia Trial

Sentenced to life imprisonment for sabotage and conspiracy to overthrow the state.

1990
Release from Prison

Released after 27 years in prison, becoming a global symbol of freedom and justice.

1993
Nobel Peace Prize

Awarded Nobel Peace Prize with F.W. de Klerk for their efforts to end apartheid peacefully.

1994
Elected President

Became South Africa's first black president in the country's first fully democratic elections.

1999
Peaceful Transition

Stepped down as president, completing a peaceful transition and focusing on humanitarian work.

2013
Global Legacy

Passed away at age 95, leaving behind a legacy of peace, reconciliation, and human dignity.
