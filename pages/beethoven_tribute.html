Ludwig van Beethoven (1770-1827) - The Deaf Genius Who Transformed Music Forever

The Musical Revolutionary

Ludwig van Beethoven was a German composer and pianist whose music is amongst the most performed of the classical music repertoire. He remains one of the most admired composers in the history of Western music, and his works rank amongst the most performed pieces in the classical music repertoire and span the transition from the classical period to the romantic era in classical music.

Despite facing progressive hearing loss that began in his twenties and eventually led to complete deafness, Beethoven continued to compose, conduct, and perform. His determination to overcome this devastating challenge for a musician resulted in some of the most powerful and emotionally profound music ever written. His influence on subsequent generations of composers is immeasurable, and his music continues to inspire and move audiences worldwide.

Timeline of Musical Milestones

1770
Birth in Bonn

Born in Bonn, Holy Roman Empire (now Germany) to Johann van Beethoven and Maria Magdalena Keverich.

1782
First Published Composition

Published his first composition, Nine Variations on a March by Ernst Christoph Dressler.

1792
Move to Vienna

Moved to Vienna to study with Joseph Haydn and establish himself as a composer and performer.

1798
Hearing Loss Begins

First noticed symptoms of hearing loss, which would progressively worsen throughout his life.

1802
Heiligenstadt Testament

Wrote a letter contemplating suicide due to his deafness but resolved to live for his art.

1804
Symphony No. 3 "Eroica"

Premiered his revolutionary Third Symphony, marking the beginning of his "Heroic" period.

1808
Symphonies No. 5 & 6

Premiered both his Fifth and Sixth symphonies in the same concert, a legendary musical evening.

1814
Opera Fidelio Success

Final version of his opera Fidelio premiered successfully after multiple revisions.

1824
Symphony No. 9 Premiere

Conducted the premiere of his Ninth Symphony with "Ode to Joy," despite being completely deaf.

1827
Death in Vienna

Died at age 56 in Vienna during a thunderstorm, with over 20,000 people attending his funeral.
