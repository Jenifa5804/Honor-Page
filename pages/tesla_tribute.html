Nikola Tesla (1856-1943) - The Man Who Electrified the World

The Genius of Electricity

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system. Born and raised in the Austrian Empire, Tesla studied engineering and physics but never completed his degree.

Tesla's inventions and theoretical work formed the basis of modern AC electrical systems, including the polyphase power distribution systems and the AC motor, with which he helped usher in the Second Industrial Revolution. His visionary ideas about wireless communication, robotics, and renewable energy were decades ahead of his time.
