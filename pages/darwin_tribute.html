Charles Darwin (1809-1882) - The Naturalist Who Changed Our Understanding of Life

The Evolution Pioneer

Charles Robert Darwin was an English naturalist, geologist, and biologist, best known for his contributions to the science of evolution. His proposition that all species of life have descended over time from common ancestors is now widely accepted and considered a fundamental concept in science. His theory of natural selection came to be known as Darwinism.

Darwin's scientific discovery is the unifying theory of the life sciences, explaining the diversity of life. His five-year voyage on HMS Beagle established him as an eminent geologist whose observations and theories supported Charles Lyell's conception of gradual geological change. His research on plants and animals during the voyage led to his groundbreaking insights about evolution through natural selection.

Major Scientific Contributions

Natural Selection

The process by which organisms with favorable traits survive and reproduce more successfully.

Common Descent

All species descended from common ancestors through gradual modification over time.

Sexual Selection

Evolution driven by competition for mates and mate choice preferences.

Adaptation

Traits that enhance survival and reproduction become more common in populations.

Variation

Individual differences within species provide raw material for natural selection.

Biogeography

Geographic distribution of species reflects their evolutionary history and migration patterns.

"It is not the strongest of the species that survives, nor the most intelligent, but the one most responsive to change."

- Charles Darwin

Timeline of Discoveries

1809
Birth in Shrewsbury

Born to Robert and Susannah Darwin in Shrewsbury, England, into a wealthy family.

1831
HMS Beagle Voyage Begins

Embarked on the five-year voyage as ship's naturalist, visiting South America, Australia, and the Galápagos Islands.

1838
Theory of Natural Selection

Developed his theory of evolution through natural selection after reading Malthus's essay on population.

1859
Origin of Species Published

Published "On the Origin of Species by Means of Natural Selection," revolutionizing biology.

1871
The Descent of Man

Published work on human evolution and sexual selection, applying evolution to human origins.

1872
Expression of Emotions

Published "The Expression of the Emotions in Man and Animals," founding evolutionary psychology.

1882
Death at Down House

Died at age 73 at his home in Kent, leaving behind a revolutionary understanding of life.
